Industry Averages:

Hard vs. Soft Bounces:

  • Hard Bounces: Permanent delivery failures. Invalid or non-existent email addresses cause these. You must remove them immediately from your list.
  • Soft Bounces: Temporary delivery failures. Full inboxes, server issues, or oversized messages cause these. The system may retry sending these emails.

Acceptable Bounce Rates:

  • Aim for a hard bounce rate of less than 0.5%. Consistently going over this threshold indicates poor list hygiene or major deliverability issues.
  • Monitor soft bounces closely. Understand they are often temporary.

Best Practices for Deliverability:

  • Sender Reputation: Keep a positive sender reputation. Send relevant content to engaged subscribers. Avoid spam complaints.
  • Authentication (SPF, DKIM, DMARC): Implement email authentication protocols. These verify your sender identity. They prevent spoofing and improve inbox placement. Explore deliverability best practices from experts.
  • List Cleaning: Regularly remove inactive or unengaged subscribers. A healthy list boosts your engagement benchmarks.

Healthy Growth Strategies:

  • Website Sign-ups: Integrate prominent sign-up forms on your website. Offer clear value propositions (e.g., exclusive offers, early access).
  • Check-in Forms: Capture email addresses directly during check-in. Get explicit consent.
  • Social Media Integration: Promote email sign-ups across your social channels.

Understanding Churn:

  • Unsubscribes: Guests opting out of your communications. Analyze unsubscribe reasons to find content or frequency issues.
  • Inactive Users: Subscribers who consistently do not open or click your emails. These affect your overall engagement metrics.

Re-engagement Campaigns:

  • Win-back Sequences: Architect automated sequences targeting inactive users. Offer special deals or updated content.
  • Preference Centers: Allow guests to customize their communication preferences. This reduces unsubscribes.
